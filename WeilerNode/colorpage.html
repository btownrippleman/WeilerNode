Welcome to my web page. As you can see, this page is devoted to me (John), entitled John Is Amazing. There are some who are not privy to the self-evident fact that John is amazing. These folk suffer from a condition known as Zeezeeabululah, otherwise known as Denial of John.Scientists are hard at work today devising exquisite concoctions known to ameliorate DOJ. Some day these folks should come around.

Albert Ayler once said that "Music is the healing power of the universe", and truer words have never been spoken. Music is more than sound. Sometimes, it's not even sound at all. John Cage fans will know what I'm talking about.Music makes us feel and has the ability to evoke many different emotions.I have always been interested how music affects or changes us. I'd like to share a few examples and what kind of emotions these sounds evoke.

"Healing" by Todd Rundgren is ethereal, melancholy and euphoric and at the same time. The sounds are honest about the hardships we all face, yet in the end, uplifting.

Frank Zappa's "Don't Eat the Yellow Snow" is musically complex, and crudely absurd. It's good for a laugh and for those who like to count time signatures.

Now let's talk about Black Sabbath. As their name might suggest, they must be evil and Satanic. But, o contraire. BS's music centers on fear of evil and the desire to make things right. Also, with a healthy dose of nihilism.

Wait, I'm not done with Metal. My favorite Death Metal band of the month is Monstrosity. Uber-technical (this means complex, and most importantl, fast! What emotions do they evoke? Empowerment, also great to work out to. (Which I need to get back into doing!) )

Finally, we have Black Metal. Noted for its melancholy atmosphere, a yearning for Pagan times, and sometimes misanthropy, I think it's hilarious. Much of the music itself is strangely beautiful.

But enough about me. I want to hear what you all have to say!
