John- Man of Vision, Man of Valor

John was born in Philadelphia in 19?, and moved to Parsippany, N.J., when he was 3. John discovered music on the day (or after) when Elvis Presley died (yes, John is that old). Dad was sitting in the living room chair with a portable 8-track (remember those?) player by his side, listening to Elvis. I remember the beautiful August day, with the sun shining through the windows. I noticed commotion on the TV, thousands of people milling about this beautiful mansion. Hm, something important must have happened. I don't quite remember the exact words, but I asked Dad who he was listening to, "Elvis Presley, he just died". I understood "death" to some degree, and asked, "How did he die?" "Heart attack" came the reply.

I love Elvis to this day, but it was around Junior High when John discovered the electric guitar and hard rock/heavy metal (later, jazz and progressive rock). Through high school, John became obsessed, practicing 4 to 6 hours a day, attending the Berklee summer program when he was 16, and being inspired by Randy Rhoads, Eddie Van Halen, Yngwie Malmsteen and others. Gradually, Dad began to go completely insane, and almost killed me a few times. The "insanity gene" was passed on to me. Dad contracted lymphoma, and kicked the bucket at 42. I hope I don't contract the lymphoma gene. Grunge took off and killed any potential musical career for John. Oh well, at least I have my health and good looks!

Work as Play(Tab Content)

Either John is a complete wacko, or has it all figured out (maybe a little of both.) As the heading indicates, John regards work as play. Is that not the ideal existence? Perhaps I overstate it a bit, but my social life is work, all of my friends are at work. I like what I do, the only downside is that I cannot make my own schedule. Small price to pay though.


Working Out (Tab Content)

John was never an "exercise guy". Around 5 years ago, John had a female friend, who went to the gym every day. She looked great. Whenever John was around her, he had impure thoughts, he committed lust in his heart several times, he began to..."Hey, stop that, this a family website!" Suffice it to say, John began diligently working out too, and grew to love it. It becomes addictive, that natural high that comes from exercise. John went back to school, stopped going to the gym, and became a fat bastard. One day, he shall return.


Dreaming (Tab Content)

I mentioned dreaming above, or more accurately, daydreaming. I can't remember who said it, but there is a quote, something like, "A man is what he thinks about all day". I like that, and it has always resonated with me. One does not have to "do" something in order to lead a constructive and worthwhile existence. If one can be happy living within one's own head, what's wrong with that?
